Interlaken

Interlaken is a breathtaking Swiss resort town nestled between Lake Thun and Lake Brienz, famous for its stunning alpine views, outdoor adventures, and gateway access to the majestic Jungfrau region.

Interlaken is a vibrant resort town nestled in the heart of the Bernese Oberland region of the Swiss Alps. Its name literally translates to "between the lakes" (inter lacus in Latin), as it is strategically located on an alluvial plain called the Bödeli, wedged between the stunning turquoise waters of Lake Brienz to the east and the deep blue Lake Thun to the west. Framed by the towering peaks of the Eiger, Mönch, and Jungfrau mountains, Interlaken serves as the primary transport gateway to the entire Jungfrau region, establishing itself as the Adventure Capital of Switzerland.
Interlaken's history began with an Augustinian Monastery, established around 1133, around which a small settlement grew. The town was originally known as Aarmühle until 1891, when it was officially renamed Interlaken to capitalize on its appealing geographic position and growing international reputation. This reputation began around the early 19th century, driven by landscape painters and the popularity of spa treatments. The town's growth accelerated dramatically with the introduction of steamships on the lakes (starting in 1835) and, critically, the railway lines beginning in the mid-19th century. This infrastructure allowed Interlaken to develop the grand hotels lining the Höheweg promenade, cementing its status as a major year-round destination for both relaxing tourists and mountaineering enthusiasts.

Interlaken is unique in its capacity to offer both extreme adrenaline sports and peaceful alpine exploration, making it a hub for a diverse range of visitors.
  • Adventure Sports Mecca: The region's geography—with high mountains, deep lakes, and rushing rivers—supports year-round adventure. It is world-famous for tandem paragliding and skydiving, which offer stunning aerial views over the lakes and mountains. The Lütschine River is a popular spot for white-water rafting, while the lakes are ideal for kayaking and jetboat tours.
  • Gateway to the Alps: Interlaken is the essential base for exploring the high Alps. From the town's train stations, travelers easily connect to the famous villages of Grindelwald and Lauterbrunnen, and onward to the Jungfraujoch.
  • Scenic Town Views: The town's main attraction, the Höhematte Park, is a large open space where paragliders often land, offering clear, unobstructed views of the mighty Jungfrau peak that dominates the southern horizon. For a panoramic view of the town and both lakes, visitors can take the funicular up to Harder Kulm.

Key Facts

  • Location: Bernese Oberland, between Lake Thun & Lake Brienz
  • Nickname: Switzerland's Adventure Capital
  • Original Name: Aarmühle (until 1891)
  • Local Mountain View: Harder Kulm (1,322 m)
