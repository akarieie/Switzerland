Jungfraujoch: The Top of Europe

The Jungfraujoch, often referred to as the "Top of Europe," is a breathtaking mountain pass in the Bernese Alps of Switzerland.

The Jungfraujoch is a high alpine glacial saddle that connects the Jungfrau and Mönch peaks in the Bernese Alps, situated at an altitude of 3,454 meters (11,332 ft). Though not the highest peak in Europe, it holds the distinction of having the highest railway station in Europe, earning it the renowned nickname, "The Top of Europe." The entire surrounding area is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognizing its importance as part of the Swiss Alps Jungfrau-Aletsch, home to the longest glacier in the Alps, the Aletsch Glacier.
The idea of building a railway up the mountain was first proposed by industrialist Adolf Guyer-Zeller in 1893. Construction on the incredible engineering feat began in 1896 and took 16 years to complete, finally opening in 1912.

TThe Jungfrau Railway is a cogwheel train line that runs mostly through a 7-kilometer-long tunnel carved directly out of the solid rock of the Eiger and Mönch mountains. This allowed the railway to safely ascend 1,400 meters of altitude, overcoming immense financial and technical challenges. This monumental project opened up the world of permanent snow and ice to visitors from around the globe.

The Jungfraujoch offers a unique, accessible high-altitude experience that requires no mountaineering skill, making it one of Switzerland's most popular attractions.
  • Aletsch Glacier Views: From the various viewing platforms, visitors get spectacular views of the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps, stretching over 23 kilometers.
  • Sphinx Observatory An elevator whisks visitors to the Sphinx Terrace, one of the highest outdoor viewing platforms in Europe (3,571m), offering clear-day views that can stretch into France, Germany, and Italy.
  • Ice Palace: Carved into the heart of the glacier, the Ice Palace features intricate ice sculptures and tunnels, providing a surreal experience of walking inside a glacier.

The journey itself, culminating at the highest train station on the continent, is considered a scenic and historic highlight of any Swiss trip.

Key Facts

  • Location: Bernese Alps
  • Altitude: 3,454 m (11,332 ft) above sea level
  • Highest Railway Station: 3,454 m (Jungfraujoch station)
  • Highest Observation Deck: 3,571 m (Sphinx Terrace)
  • Railway Opening Year: 1912 (took 16 years to build)
  • Railway Length: The line is 9.3 km long, with 7 km inside a tunnel
  • Longest Glacier: Views of the Aletsch Glacier (approx. 23 km)
