Zurich

The Rhine Falls, Europe's largest and most powerful waterfall, is a breathtaking natural wonder where the mighty Rhine River cascades with incredible force amid stunning Swiss scenery.

Zurich is Switzerland's largest city and the capital of the canton of Zurich, renowned globally as a financial powerhouse and consistently ranked among the world's cities with the highest quality of life. Strategically located at the northwestern end of Lake Zurich (Zürichsee), where the Limmat River flows out, the city effortlessly combines a modern, cosmopolitan atmosphere with a well-preserved medieval Old Town (Altstadt) and accessible natural beauty. It is a major hub for rail and air traffic, serving as a gateway to the entire country.
Zurich's history began over 2,000 years ago, originating as the Roman customs post called Turicum around 58 BCE, built on the elevated Lindenhof Hill. By the Middle Ages, the city gained political autonomy and, in 1351, joined the Swiss Confederation. Its history was profoundly shaped in the 16th century when it became the starting point of the Swiss Protestant Reformation, led by the theologian Huldrych Zwingli, who preached at the iconic Grossmünster Church. This transformation led to a major influx of Protestant refugees, which helped turn Zurich into a center for the textile industry and, later, a modern industrial and financial capital. The city's democratic principles, formalized in the 19th century, served as a model for modern Swiss democracy.

Zurich offers visitors a perfect balance of high culture, high finance, and outdoor recreation, all within a compact and beautifully organized cityscape.
  • Historic Walkways: The Old Town (Altstadt) features narrow, winding streets, historic guild houses along the Limmatquai, and famous churches. The twin-towered Grossmünster and the Fraumünster, known for its breathtaking stained-glass windows by Marc Chagall, are must-see landmarks.
  • Culture and Shopping: The city is home to the prestigious Bahnhofstrasse, one of the most exclusive and expensive shopping streets in the world. For art enthusiasts, the Kunsthaus Zürich is one of Europe's top art museums, boasting significant collections from the Dada movement (which was born in Zurich) to Impressionism.
  • Water and Views: The city revolves around the water. Visitors can take boat cruises on Lake Zurich or even swim in the clean Limmat River during summer months. For the best panorama of the city, the lake, and the distant Alps, visitors take the mountain railway up to the Uetliberg, often referred to as "Zurich's local mountain."

Key Facts

  • Location: Northern Switzerland, on Lake Zurich
  • Significance: Switzerland's largest city and financial center
  • Historical Role: Center of the Swiss Protestant Reformation
